# Module 7 — Communication & Media Strategy Synthesis

**Role in the system:** the heart. Converts five analyses into one strategy — a thesis, a meaning architecture, and an operating system — through choice, which means through refusal.

## Purpose

Synthesize, which is not summarize. The five upstream analyses describe a possibility space; this module collapses it into a bet. A strategy is defined by what it refuses: audiences not served, meanings not claimed, channels not fed, money not chased. If the document could absorb one more idea without breaking, it is not yet a strategy.

## Invoke when

The synthesis gate is satisfied — Ontology Brief, Material Basis + Leverage Map, Cultural Field Map + Belief Delta, Behavior Model, and Semiotic System all exist with ledger tags (at proportional depth). Never before: synthesis run on partial inputs silently substitutes stereotype for the missing analysis.

## Required inputs

All five module outputs above, the live ledger, and intake's decision context (what this strategy must get decided).

## Expected outputs — the Strategy Document (assembled per `artifact-structure.md`)

1. **The strategic thesis.** One paragraph, falsifiable, in the causal-chain form: *Because [cultural/economic ground], if we make [audience] [believe/feel X], they will [behavior Y], producing [economic outcome Z] — which we will see first in [leading indicator].* Every subsequent section must be traceable to this spine.
2. **Communication objectives.** Few. Each one a job with an economic warrant (a leverage-map row) and an owner. An objective without a warrant is deleted, however inspiring.
3. **Audience logic.** Who, prioritized by value pool × belief mobility — the people worth the most whose beliefs are most movable. Includes who is deliberately *not* served.
4. **Positioning.** The meaning claim, in the topology the case earned (see form selection below): what we mean, to whom, against what, and what we refuse to mean.
5. **Narrative architecture.** The story system built on Module 3's grammar: the recurring tension the brand resolves, how individual stories ladder to the claim.
6. **Messaging system.** Hierarchy from claim to proof points, by audience and situation — with the forbidden-claims register enforced.
7. **Media logic.** Channel *roles*, not channel lists: what job each channel performs in the causal chain, why this mix serves reach/precision economics from Module 6's physics, what each channel is explicitly not asked to do. Owned/earned/paid logic stated as economics, not habit.
8. **Repetition structure.** The chosen compounding mechanism — franchises, codes, ritual calendar, cadence, community practice — per the fit tests.
9. **Operating model.** Always-on versus campaign rhythm, governance, tempo, decision rights: who may vary what, and what is inviolable.
10. **Strategic tradeoffs.** Named refusals with reasons — mandatory. Plus risks (from the ledger's fragility map) and the strategy's known failure modes.

**Form selection and parametrization.** Before drafting sections 4, 8, and the objective structure, apply `theory.md`: the territories/franchises/functions pattern is the default grammar — express the strategy in it wherever its invariants hold, because communicability, the planning matrix, and franchise generativity are strategic assets. Then *derive every parameter from this case*: territory count and carving, pillar coupling, function taxonomy (with its theory commitment tagged in the ledger — funnel vs. memory-refresh changes what Module 9 measures), franchise count cap from production capacity. Where both territories and functions are selected, build the planning matrix per `matrix-instrument.md`. Record forms, parameters, derivations, and any deviations from the grammar in the **Form Declaration** (artifact appendix) — the deliverable that proves the system derived rather than templated.

## Quality bar

The **sacrifice test**: at least three real refusals with reasons. The **spine test**: pick any section at random; its content traces to the thesis without new assumptions appearing. The **swap test**: replace the brand name and the strategy breaks. Co-developed with Module 9 — thesis and falsification conditions written in the same sitting.

## Definition of success

An intelligent team that disagrees with the strategy can still execute it faithfully, because the choices are explicit and the reasons recorded. The audit gate (Module 2) finds bets, not surprises.

## Failure modes

- **Strategy-as-summary.** The analyses restated in order, choosing nothing.
- **The everything strategy.** All audiences, all channels, all meanings — a budget allocation, not a strategy.
- **Frankenstein coherence.** Each section locally fine; no causal spine connecting them.
- **Parameter copying.** The grammar used fluently but with someone else's parameters — a precedent case's carving, taxonomy, cadence, or count caps imported into different math. The Form Declaration's derivations exist to catch exactly this.
- **Media afterthought.** Brilliant meaning architecture, then "and we'll be on social." Media logic is strategy, not fulfillment.
- **Podium foreshadowing.** Writing what will present well rather than what is true; that corruption belongs to no module.

## Never do

- Never introduce a claim absent from the ledger — synthesis combines; it does not manufacture evidence.
- Never set an objective without a leverage-map warrant, or target a behavior Module 5 marked unreachable.
- Never resolve an upstream contradiction by prose. Resolve it by decision, recorded in tradeoffs.

## Handoff

To Module 9 continuously (co-development), then to Module 2's audit gate. Only an audited strategy reaches Module 8. Backflow rights: synthesis may reopen any upstream module when inputs prove insufficient — reopening is recorded in the ledger.
