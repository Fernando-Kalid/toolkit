# Example Activation — Two Cases, Two Shapes

Two compressed activations showing the same invariants taking different forms. If these two strategies had come out shaped alike, the system would have failed its own anti-template clause.

---

## Case A — Cielo Roto, artisanal mezcal entering the US premium market

**Intake.** Layer: brand, inside an export business model. Decision context: US launch strategy for founder + importing partner; artifact audience: the partner's investment committee. Materials: production costs, two markets' distributor terms, category reports, no consumer research. Target price $65.

**Module traces (compressed):**

- **1 Ontology.** Not a beverage brand — a terroir-and-labor luxury good whose natural unit of meaning is the producer-batch. Sells: agave spirit. Represents: discernment, and contact with pre-industrial time. Is not: a tequila alternative; a party spirit. Category move: *reframe* — from "smoky tequila cousin" to "wine-grammar craft spirit." Cost of the move: education.
- **6 Commercial.** COGS makes a volume play impossible; the entire strategy is pricing power (conversion mechanism #1). Value pool: top cocktail bars in two cities — on-premise advocacy drives retail velocity at full margin. Leverage row L1: *hold $65 at velocity ← bartenders retell the batch story ← trade, not consumers first*. Negative space: distribution gaps are the importer's problem; comms will not be asked to out-shout missing shelf presence.
- **2 Ledger (sample).** K: category growth, import economics. A: US premium drinkers can be taught to distinguish artisanal from industrial mezcal (validation: bar-staff interviews, week 2). S: "smoky = quality" — quarantined.
- **4 Superstructure.** Tension with standing to engage: authenticity hunger versus extraction guilt — US drinkers want "the real thing" and fear being tourists of it. Status game: quiet connoisseurship (knowing the producer's name beats owning the expensive bottle). Belief delta: "mezcal = smoky tequila" → "mezcal = single-batch craft with a name attached" — *reframe*, riding the existing craft-spirits myth rather than replacing anything. Cheap delta; the strategy budgets accordingly.
- **5 Behavior.** Target behavior TB1: bartender puts Cielo Roto in a named menu cocktail within 90 days of first tasting. Consumer trial is downstream of that. Frictions: shelf-level pronunciation anxiety (social); bartenders' fatigue with "authentic" pitches (identity — they are the discerning ones, not the brand). Lever: give bartenders connoisseurship currency — batch dossiers they can retell as their own knowledge. Transparency test: passes; the mechanism *is* the value.
- **3 Semiotics.** Break the category codes: no skulls, no desert mysticism, no kitsch Mexico. Adopt wine grammar: producer's name on the front label, batch and village as vintage and appellation. Ritual: the copita pour with one sentence about the maker. Asset estate to build: the producer-name typography.
- **7 Synthesis — thesis.** *Because premium US drinkers buy discernment rather than smoke (F-map) and margin math forbids volume (L1), if we make the top-bar trade treat the producer-batch as the unit of meaning, bartenders will place and retell it (TB1), letting us hold $65 at target velocity — visible first in named menu placements.*
- **9 Metrics.** Leading: menu placements; bartender unprompted recall of producer names. Lagging: full-margin velocity, price maintenance (no discount dependence). Kill criterion: if after two markets fewer than a set share of trained bartenders retell the batch story unprompted, the thesis fails at the *meaning* link — reopen 3 and 5, not the media plan.

**Form Declaration.** Territories: **n=2** — *craft provenance* and *the ritual of attention*; carving derived from the trade/end-drinker meaning split (two audiences, two fronts, one claim); pillar-coupled for trade only (monthly production model). Functions: **taxonomy of two, derived from L1** — justify the premium; convert the gatekeepers. Theory commitment: trade-advocacy funnel (ledger-tagged; Module 9 measures stage conversion for trade, availability for consumers). Franchises: **n=1** — the batch-dossier series; cap derivation: founder-plus-one team, monthly cadence. Matrix: 2×2 per `matrix-instrument.md`; *ritual-of-attention × convert-gatekeepers* deliberately empty — bartenders convert on provenance, not ceremony.

## Case B — Ledgerline, B2B payroll infrastructure API

**Intake.** Layer: business model (usage-priced API). Decision context: seed-to-A growth story; audience: founding team. Buying reality: engineering champion, CFO buyer, compliance blocker.

**Module traces (compressed):**

- **1 Ontology.** Plumbing that must never be interesting. Sells: payroll rails. Represents: the relief of a solved problem — *the boring one that never fails*. Category move: accept.
- **6 Commercial.** Value pool: mid-market platforms embedding payroll; retention economics dominate — the strategy serves conversion efficiency and retention, not fame. L1: *shorten sales cycles ← compliance blocker pre-neutralized ← CFO/compliance read proof before the meeting*.
- **4 Superstructure.** The buying committee's ideology: "nobody gets fired for the incumbent." Status game: engineers signal taste by what they *don't* have to babysit. Belief delta: "startups are risky rails" → "this startup is the reliability choice" — *reframe via evidence*, not persuasion.
- **5 Behavior.** TB1: champion forwards the uptime/postmortem report internally without being asked. Friction: champions can't spend credibility on unproven vendors — the lever is giving them forwardable proof, not enthusiasm.
- **3 Semiotics.** Adopt the codes of established infrastructure (documentation aesthetics, changelog sobriety); break the startup codes (no confetti, no manifesto). The absence of marketing *is* the sign system.
- **7 Synthesis — thesis.** *Because committee buying punishes risk, if we make champions able to prove reliability internally (TB1), sales cycles shorten and logos compound — visible first in inbound citing the postmortems.*

**Form Declaration.** Territories: **n=1** — a single meaning territory ("the boring one that never fails"), not pillar-coupled: more rows would dilute the spear, and there is no periodic content model to feed. Functions: **stakeholder-jobs as columns** — champion: forwardable proof; CFO: cost-of-failure math; compliance: pre-answered audit pack. Theory commitment: committee-sale evidence chain, not a consumer funnel. Franchises: **n=0** — derivation: paid-plus-founder-led acquisition and trust economics reward a *proof cadence* (uptime reports, public postmortems, implementation notes) over episodic formats. Matrix: **1×3 degenerate form** — the grammar scales down; the strategy stays legible on one page.

---

**The point.** Same five invariants, same grammar, different parameters — all derived. Case A earned a 2×2 matrix, a pillar coupling, and one franchise; Case B earned a 1×3 stakeholder matrix, no pillars, and a proof cadence. Both strategies speak the house language, and neither could wear the other's numbers. The Form Declarations show the system deriving structure, not inheriting it.
