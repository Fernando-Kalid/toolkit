# Quality Rubric — Evaluating a Communication & Media Strategy

Score each dimension 1–5. Anchors given at 1 / 3 / 5.

**Ship rule:** no dimension below 3; average ≥ 4.0; Causal Integrity and Commercial Traceability both ≥ 4. A failing score names the module to reopen — the rubric is a diagnostic, not a verdict.

## Dimensions

**1. Causal Integrity** *(reopens Module 7)*
- 1 — Objectives and tactics coexist without a causal account; the "strategy" is a themed budget.
- 3 — A thesis exists; most sections trace to it, some free-float.
- 5 — One falsifiable chain runs exposure→meaning→behavior→money; any section sampled at random traces to it without new assumptions appearing.

**2. Commercial Traceability — the no-orphan rule** *(reopens 6 or 7)*
- 1 — Symbolic work floats free of economics; "brand love" as terminal goal.
- 3 — Most symbolic investments name a conversion mechanism; some are decoration.
- 5 — Every meaning claim traces to a conversion mechanism and a leverage-map row; every commercial objective traces back to a feasible meaning/behavior change.

**3. Cultural Specificity — the swap test** *(reopens 4)*
- 1 — Findings are horoscopes; swap the brand name and nothing breaks.
- 3 — Real cultural observation, unevenly specific to this market.
- 5 — The cultural analysis could only describe this market and this entity; the brand's own standing in the field is honestly located.

**4. Decisional Content — the sacrifice test** *(reopens 7)*
- 1 — Nothing refused; all audiences, all channels, all meanings.
- 3 — Some priorities; refusals implied rather than owned.
- 5 — At least three explicit, reasoned refusals; rejected alternatives documented; the strategy is recognizable by what it declines.

**5. Epistemic Honesty** *(reopens 2)*
- 1 — Assumptions read as facts; confidence is a property of the prose.
- 3 — A ledger exists; tagging is uneven or drifted between drafts.
- 5 — Load-bearing claims tagged with evidence classes; fragility mapped; exposures signed; forbidden claims policed; the audit drew blood and the strategy survived.

**6. Operational Sufficiency — the Monday-morning test** *(reopens 7)*
- 1 — Inspiring, unexecutable; no one knows what to do first.
- 3 — Channel roles and cadence exist; governance and decision rights vague.
- 5 — A competent team that *disagrees* could still execute faithfully: roles, tempo, decision rights, and inviolables are explicit.

**7. Distinctiveness & Memorability** *(reopens 3)*
- 1 — Category dress code fully adopted; the strategy builds someone else's memory structure.
- 3 — Some ownable assets; code strategy implicit.
- 5 — Signs with jobs, a deliberate adopt/adapt/break balance, an asset estate protected from novelty churn, and a repetition structure that compounds.

**8. Executive Legibility — the CFO test** *(reopens 8)*
- 1 — The ask is buried or absent; money arrives after meaning, if at all.
- 3 — Clear summary and ask; investment rationale thin; lag structure unstated.
- 5 — Approve/fund/protect each explicit; do-nothing cost stated; lag expectations pre-agreed; the toughest financial reader finds the chain from spend to value.

**9. Ethical Soundness — the transparency test** *(reopens 5, or kills the lever)*
- 1 — Levers depend on the audience not understanding them; anxiety is manufactured to sell relief.
- 3 — Nothing egregious; screens not documented.
- 5 — Every lever passes transparency and journalist tests on the record; promises do not exceed material delivery; value capture rides on real value exchange.

## The named tests, collected

- **Swap test** — replace the brand name; if nothing breaks, it's generic.
- **Derivation test** — every form parameter (territory carving and count, function taxonomy, franchise caps, cadence) traces to a fact of this case, not to a precedent case.
- **Sacrifice test** — three real refusals with reasons, or it isn't a strategy.
- **Spine test** — random section traces to the thesis, or synthesis failed.
- **Stranger test** — an intelligent outsider can say what the entity is in one paragraph.
- **Monday-morning test** — a team knows what to do first, and what they may not vary.
- **CFO test** — the causal chain from spend to value survives the toughest financial reader.
- **Transparency test** — every influence lever survives being explained to its target.
- **Month-3 test** — the strategy has pre-agreed what the dashboard will show before effects arrive, so early silence doesn't kill a working strategy.
