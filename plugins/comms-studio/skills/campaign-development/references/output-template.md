# The Campaign Document — structure and templates

One markdown file: `campaign-<brand-or-campaign-slug>.md`, in the user's working language. Sections may shrink to tight paragraphs; they may not silently disappear — an explicitly empty section with a reason is information, a missing one is a hole. Every section traces to a step; a section with no upstream step has no right to exist.

## Structure

**0 · The campaign in one paragraph** — objective, belief-move, the idea, the hero moment, the number, the dates. Written last, placed first. A reader who stops here knows what is being attempted and what would prove it worked.

**1 · Brief card** *(Step 1 + Gate A)* — template C1, filled. Record the Gate A decision: user-confirmed, or a declared [S] choice with reasoning.

**2 · The insight** *(Step 2)* — the one sentence, the tension it names, where in the strategy (or verbatims) it was mined, and the opposite test shown: insight / opposite / why both are arguable.

**3 · The platform** *(Step 3 + Gate B)* — the one-sentence platform; which existing distinctive asset it extends and how; the franchise logic (why the third run is richer than the first); three concrete example executions. Record the Gate B decision and the candidates it beat.

**4 · Test scorecard** *(Step 4)* — template C2, completed, one line of evidence per verdict. Include any repair history (what failed on first pass, how it was repaired).

**5 · The ecosystem** *(Step 5)* — channel-role table (channel · its one job · cadence); brand-building vs. activation split with the reasoning behind the chosen ratio; hero–hub–hygiene structure; where the resource is concentrated and what orbits.

**6 · The arc** *(Step 5)* — tease → reveal → sustain → convert → afterglow with dates (collapse phases if the case argues for it, and say why); the **first domino**, named, with its date.

**7 · Measurement** *(Step 6)* — template C3, completed: the lagging number, leading indicators, amplify-if and kill/rework-if conditions with thresholds and dates. Plus: which result would warrant reopening which part of the strategy.

**8 · Scope and deliverables** *(Step 6)* — the full deliverable list, sized to the named team, with owners if known; and **the cuts** — what was deliberately not made. The cuts are evidence the concentration is real.

**9 · The ledger** — load-bearing claims with K/A/I/S tags; declared assumptions (each with what would validate it); gate decisions and their basis; any deviations from the methodology and why.

**Appendix · Rejected candidates** — the platforms that failed or lost at Gate B, with their scorecards. Trust lives here: a document that shows what it refused is credible in a way a highlight reel is not.

**Final page · Implementation checklist** — the eleven lines below, checked honestly. An unchecked box with a reason beats a falsely checked one.


## Implementation checklist

```
 1. ☐ Brief on a card — one objective, one segment, one territory, one arc,
      falsifiable belief-move, one number
 2. ☐ Insight sentence — one tension/job, survives the opposite test
 3. ☐ Idea as a franchise — one-sentence platform + 3 example executions;
      extends an existing distinctive asset
 4. ☐ Tests passed — all seven, in writing
 5. ☐ Register check — mode of astonishment matches the brand
 6. ☐ Channel roles assigned — each channel has one job
 7. ☐ Resource concentrated — one hero moment named and over-funded; rest cut
 8. ☐ Arc mapped — tease → reveal → sustain → convert → afterglow, with dates
 9. ☐ First domino named — the single launch act
10. ☐ Kill + amplify criteria pre-registered — written before launch,
      with leading indicators
11. ☐ Scope protected — deliverable list executable by the actual team
```
