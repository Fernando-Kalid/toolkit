# Module 4 — Cultural & Ideological Superstructure

**Role in the system:** maps the belief field the strategy must operate in — and computes the belief delta the strategy requires.

## Purpose

Describe the ideological weather around the market: dominant beliefs, consumer myths, status games, social scripts, aspirations, anxieties, and the live cultural tensions the entity could engage. Then state precisely which beliefs must change — or be ridden as they are — for the strategy to win. Culture here is not "trends"; it is the system of meanings inside which the offer becomes desirable or invisible.

## Invoke when

Early in any full build, after Module 1's first draft; whenever a strategy will ask people to believe something new; when a user asks why a category behaves "irrationally"; before entering a market whose culture the organization does not natively inhabit.

## Required inputs

- Ontology Brief (Module 1) — what the entity claims to be tells you which beliefs are load-bearing.
- Whatever cultural evidence intake produced (research, community observation, discourse, sales anecdotes) — ledger-tagged by class. Where evidence is thin, findings are A/I by default and say so.
- Draft Leverage Map from Module 6 when available, to focus the mapping on beliefs that gate money.

## Expected outputs

**The Cultural Field Map:**

1. **Category ideology.** What "everyone knows" in this category — the defaults nobody argues because nobody notices them. These are the strategy's deepest constraints or its biggest openings.
2. **Myths in circulation.** The stories consumers already carry (the founder in the garage, the artisan untouched by commerce, the scientific breakthrough). Myths are pre-built meaning: expensive to fight, cheap to ride, dangerous to fake.
3. **Status games.** What is being signaled, by whom, to whom, in what currency — distinguishing loud status (display), quiet status (connoisseurship legible only to insiders), and virtue status (moral positioning). Specify which game the entity's buyers are playing; misreading the game is how brands compliment their customers into embarrassment.
4. **Scripts.** Generational, class, gender, and lifestyle scripts *where genuinely relevant* — with specificity or not at all. "Millennials value experiences" is not a finding; it is a horoscope.
5. **Tensions and anxieties.** The live contradictions between what the culture professes and what life delivers. Tensions are the richest strategic material: a brand that honestly resolves a real tension acquires meaning no media budget can buy.
6. **Aspirational identities.** Who people are trying to become in this domain, and what blocks the becoming.

**The Belief Delta table.** For each belief that gates the strategy: current belief → required belief → difficulty → mechanism (**ride** existing belief / **reframe** its object / **replace** it). Riding is cheap, reframing affordable, replacing expensive and slow — a strategy that requires replacing a deep belief must say so and budget accordingly. The delta is the module's decisive output; the map exists to make the delta accurate.

## Quality bar

Every finding is specific enough to be wrong — falsifiable, attributable to *this* market, evidenced or honestly tagged. The **swap test** applies: if a finding would be equally true of any category, it isn't a finding. Scripts and identity claims are handled with precision and respect, never as caricature.

## Definition of success

Module 3 designs signs into a mapped field rather than a guessed one. Module 5 knows which motivations have cultural wind behind them. Module 7's thesis states its belief delta and mechanism honestly — and the cost difference between riding and replacing shows up in the plan.

## Failure modes

- **Trend-deck generality.** Horoscope findings; culture as confetti.
- **Imported culture.** Applying one market's culture wars, class markers, or discourse to another market that doesn't share them.
- **False consensus.** Mistaking the loudest online discourse for the culture. Most of the market is not in the conversation.
- **Ideology tourism.** Attaching the brand to tensions it has no standing to engage — the field map must include *the brand's own position* in the field.
- **Tension inflation.** Declaring a cultural crisis where there is a mild preference, to justify a dramatic strategy.

## Never do

- Never recommend engaging an anxiety by amplifying it. The system's strategies resolve tensions or leave them alone; manufacturing insecurity to sell relief is outside the ethical floor (see `theory.md`, legitimacy constraint).
- Never launder an S-tag finding into the Belief Delta.
- Never flatten a culture into a demographic.

## Handoff

Complete when the Field Map covers ideology, myths, games, scripts, tensions, aspirations — and the Belief Delta names each required change with mechanism and difficulty, all tagged. Hands to Modules 3, 5, and 7. If Module 6 later shows the money concentrates in a different audience, re-derive the delta for the audience that actually pays.

## Output shape

```
CULTURAL FIELD MAP — [market, entity]
IDEOLOGY:  "everyone knows …" → constraint / opening
MYTHS:     [myth] → ride / avoid / at-risk-of-faking
STATUS:    game: loud / quiet / virtue → currency … → audience playing it …
SCRIPTS:   [script] → relevance … → evidence class …
TENSIONS:  professed X vs lived Y → brand's standing to engage: yes/no because …
ASPIRATION: becoming … blocked by …

BELIEF DELTA
current belief → required belief → difficulty → mechanism (ride/reframe/replace) → tag
```
