# Module 6 — Marketing & Commercial Analysis

**Role in the system:** establishes the material basis — how the money actually works, where value concentrates, and where communication can genuinely move a number.

## Purpose

Give the strategy an economy to serve. Describe the business model and revenue mechanics, locate the value pools, characterize the category's buying physics, and produce the module's decisive output: the **Communication Leverage Map** — an honest account of which economic outcomes communication can affect, through which behaviors, at what plausible magnitude. Equally important is the negative space: what communication *cannot* fix here.

## Invoke when

At entry of every full build, co-iterating with Module 1; when a user asks where marketing money should go or what communication is *for* in their business; when a strategy's objectives read like wishes with no account of the cash register.

## Required inputs

- Business model description, pricing, channels; unit economics as available (estimates are fine — tagged as such).
- Category data from intake: growth, concentration, purchase frequency and weight, switching behavior.
- Ontology Brief draft (Module 1) — co-iteration partner.
- Competitive set with actual economics where visible, not just share of voice.

## Expected outputs

**The Material Basis Brief:**

1. **Revenue mechanics.** How money enters: transaction, subscription, usage, licensing, intermediated. What drives each line — price × volume × frequency × retention — and which multiplier is currently the binding constraint.
2. **Value pools.** Where profit concentrates: which customers, products, channels, geographies. Averages lie; the pool view tells the truth. Identify the customers whose lifetime economics fund everything else.
3. **Category physics.** Purchase frequency and weight distribution (light-buyer versus heavy-buyer math changes everything about media), switching costs and triggers, seasonality, growth, concentration.
4. **Competitive position, economically stated.** Cost position, price position, distribution strength, pricing power status — not rhetoric about "differentiation."
5. **Acquisition and retention logic.** How customers arrive (channels, CAC where known), why they stay or leave, expansion mechanics.
6. **Constraints.** Budget realism, regulatory limits, capacity, distribution gaps, organizational maturity. A strategy priced beyond its constraints is fiction with a spreadsheet.

**The Communication Leverage Map.** Rows of the form: *economic outcome ← required behavior ← whose behavior ← plausible magnitude ← confidence (ledger tag)*. This map is the bridge every other module crosses: Module 5 targets behaviors from it, Module 7 sets objectives on it, Module 9 measures against it.

**The negative space.** Explicit list of problems communication cannot solve in this business (product deficits, price structure, distribution absence, service failures) — with backflow notes rather than silent acceptance of an impossible brief.

## Quality bar

Numbers carry tags and sources; estimates are labeled estimates. The Leverage Map contains magnitudes someone was willing to defend, not directional hand-waves. The value-pool view is specific enough to change where the money aims. The negative space is present and uncomfortable.

## Definition of success

The strategy aims symbolic investment where it can convert (see `theory.md`, conversion mechanisms). Objectives in Module 7 inherit economic warrants. Nobody downstream can claim a communication objective without pointing at a leverage-map row.

## Failure modes

- **TAM theater.** Sizing the addressable universe instead of locating the reachable pool.
- **Average-customer fiction.** Strategy built for a mean that describes no one who pays.
- **Comms omnipotence.** Accepting jobs that belong to product, price, or distribution — the strategy then fails on schedule and communication takes the blame.
- **Spreadsheet cosplay.** False precision on S-tagged numbers; three decimal places on a guess.
- **Category autopilot.** Importing another category's physics (subscription logic into occasional purchase; heavy-buyer logic into a light-buyer category).

## Never do

- Never let the strategy proceed against a value pool the business cannot serve profitably.
- Never present an estimate as a measurement — the ledger exists precisely here.
- Never size an opportunity without naming who has to do what for the money to move.

## Handoff

Complete when the Brief and Leverage Map are tagged and the negative space is written. Hands to: Module 1 (co-iteration), 4 and 5 (which audiences and behaviors matter economically), 7 (objective-setting), 9 (business KPIs). Contradictions with the claimed ontology go to the ledger as strategic risks for Module 7.
