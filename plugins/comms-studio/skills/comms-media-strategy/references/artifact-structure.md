# The Final Strategy Artifact — Structure

The Strategy Document is the system's terminal deliverable (with the executive argument as its decision-facing companion). The skeleton below is stable; **depth is proportional** — a market entry fills it as a document, a campaign fills it as tight paragraphs. Every section names its source module; a section with no upstream source has no right to exist.

## Structure

**0. Executive Summary** *(Module 8)* — situation, bet, money, ask. One page. Written last, placed first.

**1. The Entity** *(Module 1)* — what this is, its layer, its category logic and the price of that choice, what it sells versus what it represents, what it is not. The reader finishes knowing exactly what object the strategy serves.

**2. The Money** *(Module 6)* — revenue mechanics and binding constraint, value pools, category physics, competitive position in economic terms, the Communication Leverage Map, and the negative space: what communication is not being asked to fix here, and who owns those problems instead.

**3. The Field** *(Module 4)* — category ideology, myths, status games, relevant scripts, cultural tensions the entity has standing to engage, and the Belief Delta: each required belief change with its mechanism (ride / reframe / replace) and cost.

**4. The People** *(Module 5)* — current practice in the audience's own logic, target behaviors (observable, dated, valued), the friction inventory, and the screened levers with their cultural and economic warrants.

**5. The Thesis** *(Module 7)* — the falsifiable causal chain, stated in one paragraph, plus the refusals that define it. The shortest section and the one everything else must trace to.

**6. The Meaning Architecture** *(Modules 7 + 3)* — positioning in its chosen topology; the semiotic system (signs with jobs, code strategy, distinctive-asset estate); narrative architecture; the messaging system with the forbidden-claims register enforced.

**7. The Operating System** *(Module 7)* — media logic as channel roles with reach/precision economics; the repetition structure in its chosen form; campaign versus always-on rhythm; governance, tempo, and decision rights (who may vary what; what is inviolable).

**8. The Proof System** *(Module 9)* — the KPI stack mapped to the chain, lag structure, experiment agenda with decision consequences, feedback cadence, pre-registered success and failure conditions, Goodhart defenses.

**9. The Ledger** *(Module 2)* — load-bearing claims with tags and evidence classes, the fragility map, declared assumptions with validation plans or signed exposures, the forbidden-claims register, and the audit verdict.

**Appendix A. Form Declaration** *(Module 7, per `theory.md`)* — for each of territories / franchises / functions: the parameters chosen (count, carving, pillar coupling, taxonomy, caps, cadence) with their case-derivations; the theory commitment the function taxonomy encodes (ledger reference); the planning matrix where built (`matrix-instrument.md`) with its deliberately empty cells; any deviation from the default grammar with its invariant-level reason. Plus any reopened modules (backflow log).

**Appendix B. Rejected Alternatives** *(Modules 7 + 8)* — the strategic roads not taken and why. Trust lives here.

## Assembly rules

- The thesis is written before sections 6–8 are finalized; those sections implement it, not the reverse.
- No number without a tag; no tag upgraded between draft and final without a ledger entry.
- Sections may shrink to a paragraph; they may not silently disappear. An explicitly empty section ("no belief change required — the strategy rides existing belief; see Delta") is information. A missing one is a hole.
- Render as `docx` for the working document; module 8's output renders via the user's presentation skills.
