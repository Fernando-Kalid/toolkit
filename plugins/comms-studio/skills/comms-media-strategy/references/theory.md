# Theory — What Every Strategy Must Contain, and What It Must Only Sometimes Contain

Read this before the first full build, and any time you are tempted to reach for a familiar structure. It settles the system's central conceptual question and defines the doctrine that keeps the system from becoming a template.

## The question

Are *strategic territories*, *content franchises*, and *strategic functions* universal fundamentals of Communication & Media Strategy — or contingent artifacts of particular business cases?

**Verdict: each is an invariant core wearing case-specific parameters.** The cores are ancient and near-universal; the parameters are local and derived. The system therefore does two things at once: it adopts the territories/franchises/functions pattern as its **default grammar** — the standard language in which strategies are expressed — and it polices the **parameters** (how many, which carving, what cadence, what taxonomy), which must be derived from the case rather than copied from a precedent one. The historical failure of the genre is not using these forms; it is transplanting one business's parameters into another business's strategy.

## What a Communication & Media Strategy is

A Communication & Media Strategy is **a theory of how a specific organization converts meaning into money, engineered into an operating system**. Anything less collapses into one of the genre's two failure products: the media plan with no theory of meaning (distribution of nothing in particular) or the brand poem with no theory of money (meaning that never cashes out). The definition has teeth: "theory" means falsifiable; "operating system" means someone can run it Monday morning; "specific organization" means it dies if you swap the brand name.

## The five invariants

Every strategy the system produces must contain these five, in *some* form. Their shape varies by entity; their presence does not.

**1. A meaning claim.** A chosen location in cultural-semantic space: what this entity must come to mean, to whom, against which live alternatives — and what it refuses to mean. Inescapable because communication *is* the management of meaning; an entity that means nothing specific cannot be preferred, only encountered.

**2. A causal thesis.** A falsifiable chain: exposure → meaning → belief/feeling → behavior → money. Inescapable because a strategy is a bet about causation; without the chain it is decoration with a budget. The thesis is the spine — every other element must be traceable to it.

**3. A repetition structure.** The mechanism by which communication compounds into memory and expectation instead of evaporating. Inescapable because minds forget and markets churn; brand equity is accumulated through consistent, distinctive, repeated encoding — never declared into existence.

**4. A work specification.** The discrete jobs communication must perform, each tied to an economic mechanism and an owner. Inescapable because budgets are finite, jobs left unstated go undone, and jobs stated without economic warrant consume the budget of jobs that matter.

**5. A proof system.** Pre-registered conditions under which the organization would conclude the strategy is working — and conditions under which it would conclude it is failing. Inescapable because an unfalsifiable strategy is not a strategy; it is an ideology with a media budget.

## The three forms: invariant core, derived parameters

### Strategic functions — the oldest core

**Invariant core.** A single act of communication cannot simultaneously optimize for attention, usefulness, proof, and action — so pieces must be assigned differentiated jobs. This is nearly as old as rhetoric: Cicero's *docere, delectare, movere* is a function taxonomy; so is AIDA (1898); so is hero–hub–help. The differentiation of jobs is a real structural fact about audiences.

**Derived parameters.** The *taxonomy* is downstream of the value model (Module 6). A funnel-shaped lead business derives something like reach / usefulness / trust / conversion, with conversion primacy, because its economics end in an unlocked lead. A subscription business must derive retention functions. A CPG brand may need almost none of the funnel shape.

**The theory commitment — record it in the ledger.** Any funnel-shaped taxonomy encodes a contested theory of how advertising works. The Ehrenberg-Bass school denies that buyers traverse funnels at all: advertising works by refreshing memory structures, under which functions collapse into reach plus distinctiveness. Note carefully what survives that collapse: the *grammar* — reach + distinctiveness is itself a two-function taxonomy. The functions language is theory-agnostic notation; the specific taxonomy is a theoretical commitment. Tag the commitment (funnel vs. memory-refresh, or a hybrid) in the ledger, because it changes what Module 9 must measure — stage-conversion rates versus availability and asset-attribution tracking — and what would falsify the thesis.

### Strategic territories — bounded scope, two objects often conflated

**Invariant core.** Memory and production capacity are finite; a brand that talks about everything is remembered for nothing. This is positioning theory and category-entry-point research, and it holds almost everywhere.

**Distinguish two objects.** A **meaning territory** is a bounded claim in cultural space — near-universal; even a crisis communicator or a final-week political campaign bounds itself to the two or three things it will say. A **content pillar** is a meaning territory *coupled to periodic production cadence* — an artifact of feed-era publishing, useless to the brand that makes one film a year. Most strategy documents blur these; this system never does. Territories are strategic; pillar-coupling is an operational parameter that must be earned by a periodic production model.

**Derived parameters.** Count (1…n — a single position is a one-territory case; the grammar degrades gracefully). Pillar coupling (yes/no, from the production model). The specific carving — derived from where proof, audience, and business model intersect. A carving can exist only because two objects coincide in one business (the flagship proof object and the B2B marketing object being the same thing, say); change the business model and that carving dissolves while the bounded-scope constraint survives untouched.

### Content franchises — serialization economics in period costume

**Invariant core.** Repetition amortizes production cost and builds audience habit. Dickens published in installments; the newspaper column is a franchise; classical rhetoric's fixed genres were proto-franchises.

**Derived parameters.** The modern form — hook conventions, "training the algorithm" — is a creature of feed-era platforms and small teams. Count caps derive from production capacity: a max-of-seven rule exists because a team is two people (hours per episode × cadence must fit inside available hours); a forty-person newsroom has different math; a paid-only acquisition strategy may need no franchises at all.

**Generative note.** Franchises are also a creativity technology: a container brief ("episode 12 of X") reliably outperforms a blank page, and formats give non-creative stakeholders a handle on creative work. This generativity is part of their adoption value, counted below.

## Why the pattern is the default grammar

Three properties earn territories × functions × franchises the status of house language, wherever their invariants apply:

1. **Communicability.** The pattern is visual, teachable, and survives transmission through an organization without a strategist attached. A strategy the whole company can hold in its head outperforms a bespoke ontology that is 10% more precise and 100% dependent on its author.
2. **The matrix.** Territories × functions produces the strategy's central planning surface — every piece of communication gets a cell address, gaps and waste become visible, and briefs write themselves. See `matrix-instrument.md` for the full instrument.
3. **Generativity.** Franchises convert strategy into repeatable creative production; constraint feeds invention.

**And the limit:** adoption value never overrides a failed *invariant-level* fit. The one-film-a-year brand gets meaning territories but no pillars; the crisis communicator gets bounded claims and stakeholder jobs, not a content matrix; the E-B-committed brand gets a two-function taxonomy, not a funnel. The grammar bends to the case — never the reverse.

## Do not overfit — doctrine

1. **Form follows thesis.** Express the strategy in the default grammar only after the causal thesis exists. A form chosen first will quietly write the strategy for you.
2. **Parameters are derived, never copied.** The overfit risk lives mainly at the parameter level: importing another case's carving, count caps, cadence, or function taxonomy into a business with different math. Every parameter in the Form Declaration carries its derivation — the **derivation test**: each count, carving, cadence, and taxonomy traces to a fact of *this* case, not to a precedent case.
3. **The null form is legitimate.** A strategy may be one position, one job, one repetition mechanism, one metric that matters. Sparse is not underdeveloped; sparse is decided — and still expressible in the grammar (a 1×2 matrix is a matrix).
4. **Form Declaration is mandatory.** The final artifact records the forms used, their parameters with derivations, the theory commitments they encode, and any deviations from the default grammar with reasons (see `artifact-structure.md`). Structure becomes a decision with an audit trail, not a habit.
5. **Warning signs of overfit:** territories that exclude nothing; pillar-coupling without a periodic production model; franchises invented to fill a template slot or capped by someone else's team size; functions that mirror departments or a precedent funnel; any structure that changes no allocation, no sequence, and no sacrifice.
6. **Anti-template clause, restated.** Sharing a grammar across strategies is a feature; sharing parameters across different businesses is the bug. If a luxury maison, a B2B infrastructure vendor, and a public-health campaign emerge with the same carving, the same taxonomy, and the same cadence, the system has failed — however fluent the language.

## Material basis and ideological superstructure

The strategy operates on two connected planes. The **material basis**: how the organization actually makes money — revenue mechanics, margin structure, value pools, acquisition and retention economics, constraints. The **ideological superstructure**: the beliefs, myths, identities, status games, anxieties, and social scripts within which the offer becomes meaningful and desirable. The system's defining discipline is that it never lets either plane float free of the other.

**The conversion mechanisms.** Symbolic work converts to economic value through nameable channels, and every symbolic investment in a strategy must name its channel:

- **Pricing power** — meaning raises willingness to pay; the margin between commodity price and brand price is rented from the superstructure.
- **Demand creation** — mental availability at category entry points; being the option that comes to mind when the buying situation arises.
- **Conversion efficiency** — trust and legibility lower friction, objection, and acquisition cost.
- **Retention and expansion** — identity adoption and ritual make leaving costly in self-concept, not just switching cost.
- **Talent, partners, distribution** — meaning recruits collaborators on better terms.
- **License to operate** — cultural legitimacy lowers regulatory, activist, and reputational tax.
- **Capital narrative** — the story investors hold changes the price and patience of money.

**The no-orphan rule (two-way traceability).** Every semiotic or cultural claim in the strategy must trace forward to a conversion mechanism; every commercial objective must trace back to a feasible change in meaning or behavior. Symbols without economics are art. Economics without symbols is arithmetic. Neither is strategy. The parameter derivations above are this rule applied to structure: a function taxonomy traces to revenue mechanics; a territory carving traces to where proof and audience meet the business model; a franchise cap traces to production economics.

**The constraint dialectic.** The base constrains the superstructure: an organization cannot durably mean what it cannot materially deliver. The promise–delivery gap accrues reputational debt, and the interest rate is set by how loudly the promise was made. But the superstructure also rewrites the base: change what a product means and you change its price, its buyer, and its margin structure. The strategy must know which direction it is working in at every point — and the epistemic ledger must record when it assumes the harder direction (changing belief) where the cheaper one (riding belief) was available.

**The legitimacy constraint.** Value capture must ride on real value exchange. The system does not produce strategies whose economics depend on deception, on manufactured anxiety, or on the audience failing to understand the mechanism being used on them. This is an ethical floor and a commercial one: illegitimate capture is short and expensive.

## Instruments, not allegiances

The system borrows instruments where they cut: Bourdieu's symbolic capital and distinction for status games and pricing power; Holt's cultural tensions for superstructure mapping; Ehrenberg-Bass memory economics for repetition, mental availability, and the standing challenge to funnel ontology; the rhetorical tradition from Cicero to AIDA for function taxonomies; the semiotic tradition for codes and signs; behavioral science for friction and lever design. No framework is load-bearing dogma, and when instruments conflict — funnel versus memory-refresh, distinctiveness versus differentiation — the causal thesis arbitrates and the ledger records which theory the strategy is betting on.
